What OTT, CTV and CATV Means

OTT (Over-The-Top) refers to streaming services that deliver video content directly over the internet without the need for traditional cable or satellite TV. Platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Disney+ are popular examples of OTT. Users can watch movies, shows, or live content anytime on smart TVs, smartphones, tablets, or laptops—completely on-demand.

CTV (Connected TV) refers to any television that connects to the internet to stream content, either through built-in smart TV features or external devices like Roku, Amazon Fire Stick, or Apple TV. CTV is a popular way people access OTT platforms. For advertisers, OTT and CTV offer targeted, trackable, and highly engaging ways to reach audiences who are no longer watching traditional TV.

CATV is a cable system that delivers TV channels and often internet and phone services to homes with better signal quality.
